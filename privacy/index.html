Privacy Policy for Twoday

Last updated: March 2026

Twoday is designed to be simple, calm, and respectful of your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information the app accesses and how it is used.

Calendar Access

When Twoday is launched for the first time, macOS will ask for permission to access your calendar.

If permission is granted, Twoday reads your calendar events in order to display them within the app. This access is read-only. Twoday does not create, edit, move, or delete any calendar events.

Calendar data is accessed only on your device and is used solely to display your schedule for today and tomorrow.

Data Collection

Twoday does not collect, store, or transmit personal information.

The app does not collect:

  • Personal information
  • Calendar data
  • Location data
  • Contact information
  • Usage analytics
  • Device identifiers
  • Diagnostic data

All information used by the app remains on your device.

Data Storage

Twoday does not store calendar information internally. The app simply reads events from the system calendar when it needs to display them.

All event creation, editing, and synchronization continue to occur within Apple Calendar or the calendar services connected to it.

Tracking

Twoday does not track users across apps or websites.

The app does not include advertising frameworks, analytics systems, or third-party tracking technologies.

Third-Party Services

Twoday does not use advertising networks, analytics providers, or external data services.

Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may occasionally be updated to reflect improvements to the app. Any changes will be posted on this page.
